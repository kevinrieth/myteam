About

The Donderos

With members from all four corners of the map, The Donderos formed in 2019 to recreate the sounds of power pop and psychedelic rock heard from the 60s to today. With influences ranging from the eclectic to straight-up classics, The Donderos bring deep cuts from the Beatles, Rolling Stones, Badfinger, the Kinks–and more–back to life. With a sound that reveres the past but never binges on sentimentalism, and a setlist clad in originals and covers, The Donderos are a refreshing new arrival on the happening Chicago music scene.

Led by songwriters Kevin Rieth (bass, keys, vocals) and Jimmy Stone (guitar, vocals), The Donderos breathe new life into the Chicago music scene. Sharing vocal duties, Kevin covers territory from a howling Robert Plant to the subdued Sir Paul McCartney, while Jimmy Stone spread from a feral, young Mick Jagger to classic John Lennon. Aaron Russo (drums) rounds out harmonies while supplying chest-pounding steady beats.

The nimble and always-musical lead guitar work of Kyle Downs is equal parts Tom Verlaine and Mark Bolan with a healthy shake of Hendrix and Grant Green tossed in for good measure. The tight horn section invokes the classic Memphis sounds of Sun Studios, with Matt Mrozinski on tenor saxophone and Gary Kretchmer on trumpet, who also handles arranging duties.
